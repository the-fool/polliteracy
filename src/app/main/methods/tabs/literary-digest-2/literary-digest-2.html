Boy, Were They Wrong!

The Literary Digest published their results: Landon would win with 57% of the popular vote and an electoral college haul of 370 to 161.

Due to the stupendously large sample size of their poll, The Literary Digest firmly believed that their poll would be correct. As a matter of fact, Roosevelt won the popular vote with 61%, and swept the electoral college 523 to 8. This result differed by about 17% from the magazine's final prediction.
