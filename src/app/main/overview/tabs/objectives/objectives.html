Module Objectives

    After completing this module, you will be able to:

    • Explain the importance of randomness in a poll's sample
    • Identify whether a poll's methods lead to biased results
    • Understand how a small sample size can yet be accurate with respect to the population
    • Cite a story of a famous failed-poll example
    • Know what an "exit-poll" is and the role they play in poll forecasting
    • Grasp the role that special interest groups play in poll-gathering as well as poll reporting
    • Possess a check-list for assessing the quality of polls
    • Be able to look out for dishonest manipulation of the y-axis in reports on polls
