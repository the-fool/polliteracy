Sample size and accuracy

In this lab we will experiment with the relationship between sample size, sample randomness, and its accuracy with respect to the true population. We saw in the previous case-studies that large polls have been wrong since they were biased, and small polls could be trusted because they were sufficiently random (remember, "random" is another way of saying "fair"). And now we are going to see this principle in action with an interactive experiment.

In the first experiment, you wil take random samples of any size you choose. Notice that after your sample size grows past a certain threshold it becomes increasingly rare that your results are far off from the true population ratio.

In the second experiment, you will be confronted with a biased polling method. You can no longer take it for granted that your sample is random.
